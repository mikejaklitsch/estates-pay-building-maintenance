Estates now help pay for building maintenance. Each estate's share of the shared maintenance pool is determined by its estate power. Estate-assigned buildings (noble villas, parish churches, guild halls, etc.) are charged in full to their owning estate.

The crown pays full 100% maintenance cost (the real market price of goods consumed), up from the vanilla 20% discounted rate. Estates are charged their share directly, so the net cost to the crown is reduced by the total estate power.

[h2]Building Maintenance[/h2]
[list]
[*] The crown pays full market-price maintenance for all buildings (5x the vanilla rate)
[*] Each building's maintenance cost is calculated from its production method's goods inputs priced against the local market
[*] Shared buildings are split across estates proportional to estate power
[*] 35 estate-assigned buildings (noble villas, parish churches, guild halls, etc.) are charged in full to their owning estate
[*] Government and military buildings (royal garden, forts, council halls, etc.) are crown-funded and excluded from estate maintenance
[*] Foreign buildings use the same estate power split as domestic buildings
[/list]

[h2]Estate Tooltip[/h2]
[list]
[*] Building maintenance appears as a line item in the estate tooltip
[*] Custom in-game concepts explain how costs are calculated
[/list]

[h2]Compatibility and Performance[/h2]
[list]
[*] Performance impact is negligible after the first month. The initial tick is heavier, especially when adding to an existing save.
[*] Built for version 1.2.* and 1.3.*
[*] Mods that add new buildings with maintenance production methods will need a compatibility patch for estates to contribute to those buildings
[*] Save-game safe: can be added to existing saves without issue. Removal should also be safe, though some internal data structures may linger in the save file. Recommend making a backup beforehand.
[*] Optional submods are available to reduce the maintenance multiplier to 50% or 20% (vanilla rate) instead of the full 100%
[/list]
